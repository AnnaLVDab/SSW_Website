🏯 Origin

The origins of ramen are a blend of cultural exchange and culinary innovation.

While the dish we know today is deeply rooted in Japanese cuisine, ramen's story begins with Chinese immigrants who brought their noodle-making techniques to Japan in the late 19th century. Initially known as "shina soba" (Chinese noodles), ramen evolved significantly during the early 20th century, especially after World War II when wheat became more accessible. Over time, regional variations emerged, each with unique broths, noodles, and toppings. Ramen's journey from a humble street food to a global culinary phenomenon reflects its adaptability and the creative spirit of those who have contributed to its evolution.
